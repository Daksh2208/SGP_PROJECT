Guava Cultivation Guide

1. Seasons for Growing Guava

Guava trees thrive in tropical and subtropical climates. The ideal growing conditions for guavas are:

  • Spring: Plant guava trees in spring when temperatures are warm.
  • Summer: Ensure adequate watering and care during the hot months.
  • Autumn: Continue care; guavas may start fruiting in late summer through autumn.
  • Type: Prefers well-drained, loamy or sandy soil rich in organic matter.
  • pH Level: Optimal pH is between 5.5 and 7.0.
  • Preparation: Add compost or well-rotted manure to enhance soil fertility and drainage.

3. Best Fertilizers for Guava

  • Balanced Fertilizers: Use a balanced fertilizer with a ratio of 10-10-10 for general growth.
  • Phosphorus: Promotes flowering and fruiting. Use a phosphorus-rich fertilizer like bone meal.
  • Potassium: Enhances fruit quality. Use potassium-rich fertilizers like potassium sulfate.

Organic Options:

  • Compost: Provides essential nutrients and improves soil health.
  • Manure: Enhances soil fertility and adds organic matter.
  • Fish Emulsion: Supplies nitrogen and other nutrients for vigorous growth.

4. Common Diseases in Guava

  • Guava Wilt: Causes wilting and yellowing of leaves.
    Control: Improve soil drainage and apply fungicides.
  • Fruit Rot: Affects the fruit with soft rot.
    Control: Remove affected fruit and use appropriate fungicides.
  • Leaf Spot: Dark spots on leaves.
    Control: Use fungicides and remove infected leaves.

5. Cultural Practices for Disease Prevention

  • Proper Spacing: Ensure sufficient space between trees for good air circulation.
  • Mulching: Helps retain soil moisture and reduce disease risks.
  • Pruning: Regularly prune to maintain tree structure and remove diseased or damaged wood.
  • Water Management: Avoid waterlogging and ensure proper drainage to prevent root diseases.
