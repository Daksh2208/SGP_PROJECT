Mango Cultivation Guide

1. Seasons for Growing Mango

Mango trees grow best in tropical and subtropical climates. The ideal growing conditions are:

  • Spring: Plant mango trees in spring when temperatures are consistently warm.
  • Summer: Provide adequate water and care during the hot summer months.
  • Autumn: Maintain care; mangoes typically start flowering in late winter to early spring.

2. Soil Requirements

  • Type: Prefers well-drained, sandy loam or loamy soil.
  • pH Level: Optimal pH is between 5.5 and 7.5.
  • Preparation: Incorporate organic matter or compost to enhance soil structure and fertility.

3. Best Fertilizers for Mango

  • Balanced Fertilizers: Use a balanced fertilizer with a ratio of 10-10-10 or 8-8-8 for overall growth.
  • Potassium: Essential for fruit development. Use potassium-rich fertilizers like potassium sulfate.
  • Magnesium: Supports healthy growth and fruit quality. Use magnesium sulfate if necessary.

Organic Options:

  • Compost: Provides essential nutrients and improves soil health.
  • Manure: Enhances soil fertility and adds organic matter.
  • Fish Emulsion: Supplies nitrogen and other nutrients for vigorous growth.

4. Common Diseases in Mango

  • Powdery Mildew: White, powdery fungal growth on leaves and fruit.
    Control: Use fungicides and ensure good air circulation.
  • Anthracnose: Dark lesions on fruit and leaves.
    Control: Apply fungicides and remove affected parts.
  • Black Spot: Dark spots on mango skin.
    Control: Maintain proper sanitation and apply appropriate treatments.

5. Cultural Practices for Disease Prevention

  • Proper Spacing: Ensure sufficient space between trees for good air circulation.
  • Mulching: Helps retain soil moisture and reduce disease risks.
  • Pruning: Regularly prune to maintain tree structure and remove diseased or damaged wood.
  • Water Management: Avoid waterlogging and ensure proper drainage to prevent root diseases.
