Calcium Ammonium Nitrate (CAN): A Comprehensive Guide

Introduction

Calcium Ammonium Nitrate (CAN) is a versatile and widely used inorganic fertilizer that combines calcium and nitrogen. It provides essential nutrients to plants, promoting healthy growth, strong root development, and improved crop yields. CAN is especially valued for its balanced nutrient profile and suitability for a range of crops.

Best for Crops, Vegetables, Fruits, and Flowers

Calcium Ammonium Nitrate is effective for the following:

  • Crops: Wheat, Corn, Barley, Oats
  • Fruits: Apples, Pears, Citrus Fruits, Berries
  • Vegetables: Carrots, Onions, Lettuce, Cucumbers
  • Flowers: Marigolds, Roses, Lilies, Daisies

Soil Condition Requirements

Calcium Ammonium Nitrate is suitable for a variety of soil types. It works well in both acidic and neutral soils but should be applied with caution in alkaline soils to avoid potential imbalances. CAN helps improve soil structure and provides essential calcium and nitrogen.

How to Use Calcium Ammonium Nitrate (CAN)

  • Application Timing: Apply CAN during the growing season when plants are actively developing. It can be used both as a pre-planting and a side-dressing application.
  • Dosage:
    • For field crops: 100-150 kg per hectare, based on soil tests and crop needs.
    • For fruit trees: 80-120 kg per hectare, depending on tree size and soil fertility.
    • For vegetables: 60-100 kg per hectare, adjusted according to soil and plant requirements.
  • Application Methods:
    • Broadcast CAN evenly across the soil surface before planting and incorporate it with tillage.
    • Use side-dressing techniques during the growing season to provide additional nutrients.
    • Foliar applications can also be effective for quick nutrient uptake.
  • Watering and Activation: Ensure adequate soil moisture after application to facilitate nutrient absorption. Avoid overwatering to prevent leaching.
  • Monitoring and Adjustments: Regularly monitor plant growth and soil conditions. Adjust application rates based on crop needs and soil test results.
  • Precautions: Store CAN in a cool, dry place. Avoid applying before heavy rain to prevent nutrient runoff.

Nutrient Composition

Calcium Ammonium Nitrate provides essential nutrients for plant growth:

  • Calcium (Ca): 8-10%
  • Nitrogen (N): 25-27%

Benefits

  • Provides a balanced supply of calcium and nitrogen for optimal plant growth.
  • Improves soil structure and enhances nutrient availability.
  • Promotes strong root development and overall plant vigor.
  • Reduces the risk of calcium deficiency-related disorders in plants.
  • Versatile application methods allow for flexibility in usage.

Disadvantages

  • Excessive application can lead to nutrient imbalances in the soil.
  • Potential for leaching and runoff if applied in excess or before heavy rainfall.
  • May require careful management in alkaline soils to avoid adverse effects.

Cautions

  • Handle with care and avoid inhalation of dust.
  • Keep away from moisture and store in a sealed container.
  • Follow application guidelines to prevent overuse and potential environmental impact.

Environmental Impact

When used appropriately, Calcium Ammonium Nitrate can be beneficial for soil health and crop productivity. However, excessive use can lead to nutrient runoff and potential water contamination. To minimize environmental impact, adhere to recommended application rates and timing based on soil tests.
