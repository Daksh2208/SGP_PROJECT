Nasturtium Cultivation Guide

The Nasturtium plant is admired for its vibrant, colorful flowers and unique foliage. This guide provides detailed instructions on cultivating Nasturtiums, including the best conditions and care practices.

Optimal Growing Conditions for Nasturtium

1. Climate

  • Temperature: Nasturtiums thrive in cool to moderate temperatures ranging from 15°C to 25°C (59°F to 77°F). They can tolerate light frosts but prefer milder conditions.
  • Light: Nasturtiums do best in full sun but can also tolerate partial shade. They require at least 4-6 hours of direct sunlight each day to encourage robust flowering.
  • Humidity: They are relatively adaptable to varying humidity levels but prefer average to low humidity environments.
  • Air Circulation: Ensure good air circulation around Nasturtiums to prevent fungal diseases and support healthy growth.

2. Season

  • Planting Season: Nasturtiums are typically planted in spring after the last frost has passed. They can also be sown in late summer for a fall bloom.
  • Flowering Season: Nasturtiums generally bloom from mid-summer to fall, offering vibrant color throughout the growing season.
  • Resting Period: In colder climates, Nasturtiums may die back after the first frost. They do not usually require special care during dormancy.
  • Off-Season Care: In colder regions, consider growing Nasturtiums in containers that can be moved indoors or providing frost protection to extend their growing season.

3. Soil

  • Type: Nasturtiums prefer well-drained soil with good fertility. They thrive in a variety of soil types but do best in a loamy or sandy soil.
  • pH Level: They grow well in slightly acidic to neutral soil with a pH range of 6.0 to 7.0. Regular soil testing can help maintain the appropriate pH level.
  • Soil Preparation: Prepare the soil by incorporating organic matter or compost to enhance drainage and nutrient content.
  • Container Use: For container planting, use a high-quality potting mix with good drainage. Ensure containers have drainage holes to prevent waterlogging.

Planting and Care

4. Planting

  • Plant Selection: Choose healthy Nasturtium plants or seeds from a reputable source. Look for vigorous seedlings or seeds with a high germination rate.
  • Planting: Space plants 12-18 inches apart to allow for their mature size. Plant seeds about 1 inch deep or set seedlings at the same depth as their nursery containers.
  • Depth of Planting: Proper planting depth ensures healthy root development and promotes vigorous growth.
  • Initial Watering: Water thoroughly after planting to settle the soil and support plant establishment.

5. Watering

  • Initial Stage: Keep the soil consistently moist after planting to support root establishment. Avoid overwatering to prevent root rot.
  • Growth Stage: Water regularly during the growing season to maintain even soil moisture. Nasturtiums prefer slightly dry conditions compared to some other plants.
  • Flowering Stage: Continue to maintain soil moisture during the flowering period to support vibrant blooms and healthy foliage.
  • Dormancy Stage: Reduce watering during the dormant period or if plants are exposed to cold conditions.

6. Fertilizer

  • Chemical Fertilizers: Use a balanced, water-soluble fertilizer with a ratio of 10-10-10 or 20-20-20. Apply every 4-6 weeks during the growing season.
  • Natural Fertilizers:
    1. Compost: Incorporate compost into the soil before planting and use it as mulch to provide ongoing nutrients.
    2. Fish Emulsion: Apply fish emulsion as a liquid feed every 4-6 weeks to enhance growth and flowering.
    3. Seaweed Extract: Use seaweed extract as a foliar spray or soil drench to improve plant health and resilience.
    4. Bone Meal: Apply bone meal during planting to support root development and flowering.
  • Application Tips: Avoid applying fertilizer directly to the plant’s base to prevent burn. Water before and after applying fertilizer for best results.

Common Diseases and Their Cure

7. Powdery Mildew

Powdery mildew is a fungal disease that can affect Nasturtiums, leading to a white, powdery coating on the leaves and flowers.

  • Symptoms: White, powdery spots on foliage and flowers.
  • Prevention: Ensure good air circulation and avoid overhead watering. Remove infected plant parts promptly.
  • Natural Cure: Spray with a solution of water and baking soda (1 tablespoon per gallon) weekly until symptoms improve.
  • Chemical Cure: Apply a fungicide with sulfur or potassium bicarbonate. Follow label instructions for application.

8. Aphids

Aphids are small insects that can damage Nasturtiums by feeding on their sap.

  • Symptoms: Distorted leaves, sticky residue, and visible aphids on plants.
  • Prevention: Use insecticidal soap or neem oil to deter aphids. Encourage beneficial insects like ladybugs.
  • Natural Cure: Spray with a solution of water and insecticidal soap to remove aphids.
  • Chemical Cure: Apply an insecticide containing pyrethrin or a similar compound. Follow label instructions for correct use.

Medical Benefits of Nasturtium

Nasturtium has several traditional medicinal uses, valued especially in herbal medicine:

  • 1. Antibacterial Properties: Nasturtium extracts are used for their antibacterial effects, helping to fight infections.
  • 2. Respiratory Health: Nasturtium is traditionally used to treat respiratory conditions, including coughs and colds.
  • 3. Vitamin C Source: The plant is rich in Vitamin C, contributing to overall health and immune support.
  • 4. Skin Care: Nasturtium extracts are used in skincare products for their potential to improve skin health and reduce inflammation.
