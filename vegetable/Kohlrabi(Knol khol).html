Kohlrabi (Knol Khol) Farming Guide

1. Seasons for Growing Kohlrabi

Kohlrabi, also known as Knol Khol, is a cool-season crop. It thrives in moderate temperatures and can be grown in various seasons depending on the region:

  • Spring: Ideal for planting early in spring for a summer harvest. Kohlrabi can withstand light frosts, which can enhance its flavor.
  • Fall: Plant in late summer or early fall for a fall or winter harvest. It can tolerate cooler temperatures and mild frost.
  • Winter: In mild winter climates, kohlrabi can be grown year-round with protective measures to shield from severe cold.

2. Soil Requirements

  • Type: Kohlrabi prefers well-drained, loamy soil enriched with organic matter. Sandy loam or loam with compost works best.
  • pH Level: The optimal soil pH is between 6.0 and 7.0. Slightly acidic to neutral soil is preferred.
  • Preparation: Enrich the soil with compost or aged manure to provide essential nutrients and improve soil structure.

3. Best Fertilizers for Kohlrabi

  • Balanced Fertilizers: Use a balanced fertilizer with an NPK ratio of 10-10-10 or 14-14-14 to support overall growth.
  • Organic Options: Apply compost or well-rotted manure to provide essential nutrients and enhance soil fertility.
  • Calcium: Kohlrabi benefits from calcium for better bulb development. Use lime or calcium-rich fertilizers if needed.

4. Common Diseases in Kohlrabi

  • Clubroot: Causes swollen, distorted roots and stunted growth.
    Control: Use resistant varieties and avoid planting in infected soil.
  • Downy Mildew: Causes yellowing and downy growth on leaves.
    Control: Use fungicides and ensure good air circulation.
  • Black Rot: Leads to yellowing and wilting of leaves.
    Control: Remove affected plants and use crop rotation to prevent reoccurrence.
  • Flea Beetles: Small pests that create holes in leaves.
    Control: Use row covers or insecticidal soap to manage infestations.

5. Cultural Practices for Disease Prevention

  • Crop Rotation: Rotate crops to reduce the risk of disease buildup and avoid planting kohlrabi in the same spot year after year.
  • Proper Spacing: Ensure adequate spacing between plants to improve air circulation and reduce humidity around foliage.
  • Sanitation: Remove and dispose of plant debris and affected plants to lower disease sources.
  • Watering Techniques: Water at the base of plants to keep foliage dry and reduce the risk of fungal infections.
  • Resistant Varieties: Choose kohlrabi varieties that are resistant to common diseases for better management.

6. Growing Conditions and Care

  • Temperature: Kohlrabi grows best in cooler temperatures between 55-75°F (13-24°C). It can tolerate light frosts.
  • Watering: Keep the soil consistently moist but not waterlogged. Regular watering is essential for bulb development.
  • Spacing: Space plants 6-8 inches apart to allow for proper bulb development and good air circulation.
  • Pest Control: Monitor for pests like aphids and caterpillars. Use organic pest control methods or insecticidal soap as needed.
