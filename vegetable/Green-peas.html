Green Peas Farming Guide

1. Seasons for Growing Green Peas

Green peas are cool-season crops that thrive in moderate temperatures. The ideal growing seasons include:

  • Spring: Plant peas as soon as the soil can be worked. They can tolerate light frost, making early spring an ideal time.
  • Fall: In warmer regions, you can also plant peas in late summer to harvest in fall. Ensure they have adequate time to mature before the first frost.

2. Soil Requirements

  • Type: Peas prefer well-drained, loamy soil with good fertility. Sandy loam or clay loam soils are ideal.
  • pH Level: The ideal soil pH for green peas is between 6.0 and 7.0. Neutral to slightly acidic soil is best.
  • Preparation: Incorporate compost or well-rotted manure to improve soil fertility and structure before planting.

3. Best Fertilizers for Green Peas

  • Balanced Fertilizers: Use a balanced fertilizer with an NPK ratio of 10-10-10 to support overall growth.
  • Phosphorus: Phosphorus is essential for root development. Bone meal or a phosphorus-rich fertilizer can be beneficial.
  • Organic Options: Compost and well-rotted manure provide essential nutrients and improve soil structure.

4. Common Diseases in Green Peas

  • Powdery Mildew: A fungal disease causing white, powdery spots on leaves.
    Control: Use fungicides and avoid overhead watering.
  • Rust: Causes orange-brown pustules on leaves and stems.
    Control: Apply fungicides and remove infected plant debris.
  • Pea Weevil: Insects that feed on developing seeds, causing damage.
    Control: Use insecticides and practice crop rotation.
  • Downy Mildew: Results in yellowing and curling of leaves.
    Control: Use resistant varieties and ensure good air circulation.

5. Growing Conditions and Care

  • Temperature: Green peas grow best in temperatures between 55-75°F (13-24°C). Protect them from extreme heat and frost.
  • Watering: Keep the soil consistently moist but not waterlogged. Water deeply and regularly, especially during dry spells.
  • Support: Provide support for climbing varieties with trellises or stakes to keep plants upright and improve air circulation.
  • Pest Control: Monitor for pests such as aphids and pea moths. Use organic pesticides or introduce beneficial insects like ladybugs.
