Schemes for Welfare of Farmers in India

The Indian government has introduced several schemes to uplift farmers and improve the agricultural sector. These schemes provide financial assistance, insurance, infrastructure, and various incentives to ensure better productivity and sustainability. Below is an overview of some of the major schemes aimed at farmers' welfare.

Pradhan Mantri Kisan Samman Nidhi(PM-KISAN)

Objective:

To provide direct income support to small and marginal farmers for input costs such as seeds, fertilizers, and labor.

Launched in 2019, PM-KISAN offers an annual income support of Rs. 6,000 to eligible farmers, disbursed in three equal installments. The scheme targets small farmers to ease their financial burden.

Key Features:

  • Financial aid of Rs. 6,000 per year directly to the farmers’ bank accounts.
  • Focus on small and marginal farmers with land holdings of up to 2 hectares.
  • Provides a reliable income stream, helping farmers meet essential needs.
  • No need for intermediaries – direct transfer of funds into farmers' accounts.

Pradhan Mantri Kisan MaanDhan Yojana (PM-KMY)

Objective:

To provide social security and pension benefits to small and marginal farmers after they reach the age of 60.

Launched in 2019, PM-KMY is a pension scheme for farmers, ensuring that they have a regular income in their old age. It is targeted towards farmers with small holdings, providing them with a steady pension to support their livelihood after retirement.

Key Features:

  • Farmers between 18 and 40 years of age can enroll in the scheme.
  • Eligible farmers receive a monthly pension of ₹3,000 after turning 60.
  • The contribution amount varies depending on the age of the farmer, ranging from ₹55 to ₹200 per month.
  • The government provides an equal contribution matching the farmer's payment.
  • In the event of the farmer’s death, the spouse is entitled to receive 50% of the pension as a family pension.
  • Ensures social security for elderly farmers, reducing dependence on others post-retirement.

Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana (PMFBY)

Objective:

To provide comprehensive insurance coverage to farmers against crop failure due to natural disasters, pests, and diseases.

Introduced in 2016, PMFBY ensures farmers can recover from crop losses by providing low-cost insurance premiums. It covers a wide range of risks, including floods, droughts, and crop diseases.

Key Features:

  • Affordable premium: Farmers pay only 2% for Kharif crops, 1.5% for Rabi crops, and 5% for commercial crops.
  • Comprehensive coverage against natural calamities, pests, and diseases.
  • Encourages more sustainable farming practices by reducing risk.
  • Quick processing of claims and direct transfer of compensation to farmers' accounts.

Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchai Yojana (PMKSY)

Objective:

To improve irrigation facilities and increase the amount of arable land by promoting efficient water-use techniques.

PMKSY focuses on the effective management of water resources for agriculture. By promoting drip and sprinkler irrigation systems, the scheme ensures efficient water usage and improved irrigation facilities.

Key Features:

  • Promotes micro-irrigation technologies like drip and sprinkler systems to save water.
  • Aims to expand irrigation coverage with an emphasis on 'More Crop Per Drop.'
  • Funding for irrigation projects that develop infrastructure for better water management.
  • Encourages adoption of modern farming practices and reduces dependency on monsoon rainfall.

Modified Interest Subvention Scheme (MISS)

Objective:

To provide interest subvention to farmers on loans taken for agricultural purposes, making credit more affordable and accessible.

Introduced to help farmers manage their debt and reduce the cost of borrowing, MISS aims to boost agricultural productivity by ensuring timely access to finance.

Key Features:

  • Subvention of 2% on interest for short-term loans up to ₹3 lakh for a tenure of 1 year.
  • An additional 3% subvention for timely repayments, making the effective interest rate as low as 4%.
  • Applicable to all farmers, including marginal and small farmers.
  • Direct transfer of benefits to banks to ensure transparency and efficiency in disbursal.
  • Aims to enhance the accessibility of credit for various agricultural activities, including input costs and operational expenses.

Agriculture Infrastructure Fund (AIF)

Objective:

The Agriculture Infrastructure Fund (AIF) aims to enhance agricultural infrastructure in India by providing financial support for the development of agricultural assets. This scheme focuses on creating a robust ecosystem to increase productivity and improve the income of farmers.

Key Features:

  • Financial assistance of up to ₹2 crore at a low-interest rate for the development of projects related to agriculture and allied sectors.
  • Supports various activities, including post-harvest management, storage, and marketing infrastructure.
  • Encourages the participation of farmers, cooperatives, and agribusinesses in developing agricultural infrastructure.
  • Flexible repayment options of up to 7 years, making it easier for beneficiaries to manage their finances.
  • Promotes the use of modern technology in agriculture to improve efficiency and reduce waste.

Integrated Scheme for Agriculture Marketing (ISAM)

Objective:

To enhance agricultural marketing by providing better facilities and support for farmers, ensuring they receive fair prices for their produce.

ISAM aims to improve marketing infrastructure and promote effective marketing strategies to help farmers reach markets more efficiently.

Key Features:

  • Support for the establishment of agricultural marketing infrastructure, including market yards and cold storage.
  • Financial assistance for setting up market linkages and promoting direct sales from farmers to consumers.
  • Training and capacity-building programs for farmers on market trends and effective marketing techniques.
  • Promotion of value addition and processing of agricultural products to increase farmers' income.
  • Facilitation of technology adoption for better market access and information dissemination.

Formation & Promotion of New 10,000 FPOs

Objective:

The scheme aims to promote the formation of 10,000 new Farmer Producer Organizations (FPOs) to enhance farmers' income by providing them with a platform for collective bargaining, better market access, and shared resources.

Initiated in 2020, this scheme focuses on empowering farmers through group formation, enabling them to leverage economies of scale in production and marketing.

Key Features:

  • Support for the establishment of FPOs to foster collective farming practices.
  • Financial assistance for capacity building, training, and operational support.
  • Facilitates access to credit, technology, and markets through collaboration.
  • Promotes the use of innovative farming techniques and sustainable practices.
  • Encourages farmers to collectively negotiate better prices for their produce.

National Beekeeping and Honey Mission (NBHM)

Objective:

To promote beekeeping activities in India and increase the production of honey and other bee products.

Launched in 2020, NBHM aims to enhance the income of beekeepers and improve the productivity of crops through pollination services.

Key Features:

  • Financial assistance for setting up beekeeping units, including training and equipment support.
  • Focus on scientific beekeeping practices to improve honey yield and quality.
  • Promotion of value-added products like beeswax, propolis, and royal jelly.
  • Support for marketing and branding of honey products to increase their market value.

Market Intervention Scheme (MIS)

Objective:

The Market Intervention Scheme aims to ensure that farmers receive a fair price for their produce by intervening in the market when prices fall below a certain threshold.

Introduced to stabilize prices and ensure that farmers can sell their produce at a profit, this scheme allows government agencies to purchase surplus produce directly from farmers during periods of low prices.

Key Features:

  • Direct purchase of surplus produce by government agencies to prevent price crash.
  • Ensures minimum support prices (MSP) are met for essential crops.
  • Helps farmers get a fair price, reducing their financial risk during market fluctuations.
  • Facilitates the sale of commodities to enhance food security in the country.

Price Support Scheme (PSS)

Objective:

The Price Support Scheme is designed to provide support to farmers for specific agricultural commodities to ensure they receive adequate prices for their produce.

Launched as a mechanism to stabilize prices, this scheme ensures that farmers can sell their produce at a pre-defined minimum support price, thus securing their livelihoods.

Key Features:

  • Guaranteed minimum support price (MSP) for specified crops to protect farmers' interests.
  • Reduces dependency on market prices, ensuring income stability for farmers.
  • Promotes the production of essential crops by ensuring price security.
  • Encourages investment in agriculture by providing a safety net for farmers.

Namo Drone Didi

Objective:

The Namo Drone Didi scheme aims to empower women farmers by providing them access to drone technology for agricultural purposes. The initiative focuses on enhancing productivity and efficiency in farming through the use of modern technology.

Key Features:

  • Subsidized drones: Women farmers can access drones at a reduced cost, making technology affordable and accessible.
  • Training and support: Comprehensive training programs are provided to educate farmers on the use of drones for tasks such as spraying pesticides and monitoring crop health.
  • Enhanced productivity: Drones enable precise application of inputs, reducing waste and increasing crop yields.
  • Community involvement: The scheme encourages collaboration among women farmers, fostering a supportive network and sharing of best practices.

Rastriya Krishi Vikas Yojana (RKVY)

Objective:

The Rastriya Krishi Vikas Yojana (RKVY) aims to promote holistic growth in the agricultural sector by providing financial assistance for various agricultural projects. It focuses on increasing the productivity of agriculture, ensuring sustainable farming, and enhancing farmers' income through well-structured project reports.

Key Features:

  • Project-Based Funding: RKVY-DPR provides funding for specific agricultural projects, allowing states to develop detailed plans based on local needs.
  • Increased Investment: Encourages states to invest in agriculture and allied sectors through financial support, promoting innovation and technological advancement.
  • Focus on Infrastructure: Supports the development of agricultural infrastructure such as irrigation, storage facilities, and market access.
  • Capacity Building: Emphasizes the training of farmers and stakeholders to enhance their skills and knowledge in modern farming techniques.
  • Monitoring and Evaluation: Includes a robust system for monitoring project implementation and evaluating outcomes to ensure accountability and effectiveness.

Kisan Credit Card (KCC)

Objective:

To provide farmers with timely credit for purchasing agricultural inputs, ensuring they can meet the financial needs of their crops.

The KCC scheme offers short-term credit to farmers at a low interest rate, which can be used for farming activities or allied activities like animal husbandry and fisheries. It aims to reduce farmers' dependency on informal loans.

Key Features:

  • Short-term loans with interest as low as 2% per annum on loans up to Rs. 3 lakh.
  • Overdraft facility to meet emergency household expenses.
  • Repayment aligned with the crop cycle, making it easier for farmers to repay.
  • Coverage for personal expenses related to family needs, cattle, and fisheries.

Rainfed Area Development (RAD)

Objective:

The Rainfed Area Development (RAD) scheme aims to enhance the productivity and income of farmers in rainfed areas through sustainable agricultural practices and improved water management.

Launched to support farmers who primarily rely on rainwater for irrigation, RAD focuses on increasing the resilience of farming systems to climate variability.

Key Features:

  • Integrated watershed management to improve soil moisture and increase agricultural productivity.
  • Promotion of drought-resistant and high-yielding crop varieties.
  • Capacity building for farmers on sustainable agricultural practices and efficient water usage.
  • Financial assistance for creating irrigation facilities and soil conservation measures.
  • Linkage with other government schemes for comprehensive support and resource availability.

Per Drop More Crop (PDMC)

Objective:

The Per Drop More Crop scheme aims to promote efficient water use in agriculture through the adoption of micro-irrigation techniques such as drip and sprinkler irrigation.

Launched to enhance water-use efficiency, this scheme encourages farmers to adopt modern irrigation methods, leading to increased crop production and sustainability.

Key Features:

  • Financial assistance: The government provides subsidies for the installation of micro-irrigation systems.
  • Increased crop yield: Efficient water usage leads to improved crop health and higher yields.
  • romotion of water conservation: Encourages sustainable practices that help conserve water resources.
  • Training and support: Farmers receive training on modern irrigation practices and technologies.

Micro Irrigation Fund (MIF)

Objective:

To promote the adoption of micro irrigation techniques, such as drip and sprinkler irrigation, to enhance water-use efficiency and increase agricultural productivity.

Launched in 2019, MIF aims to provide financial support to farmers for installing micro irrigation systems, thereby helping them achieve higher yields while conserving water resources.

Key Features:

  • Low-interest loans: Financial assistance is provided at subsidized interest rates to facilitate the installation of micro irrigation systems.
  • ,Subsidy support: Farmers can receive subsidies for up to 50% of the cost of installation, making it more affordable.
  • Focus on water conservation: MIF encourages practices that conserve water and improve efficiency in irrigation.
  • Targeted assistance: Special emphasis is placed on supporting small and marginal farmers to ensure equitable access to resources.

Paramparagat Krishi Vikas Yojana (PKVY)

Objective:

To promote organic farming and increase the area under organic cultivation through traditional agricultural practices.

Launched in 2015, PKVY aims to enhance the income of farmers by adopting sustainable farming techniques and improving soil health.

Key Features:

  • Support for organic certification: Financial assistance is provided for the certification of organic produce.
  • Training and capacity building: Farmers receive training in organic farming practices and techniques.
  • Encouragement of traditional farming methods: The scheme promotes the use of indigenous seeds and traditional farming practices.
  • Financial incentives: Farmers can receive assistance for various activities, including organic inputs and marketing of organic products.

Sub-Mission on Agriculture Mechanization (SMAM)

Objective:

To promote the use of agricultural machinery and equipment to improve productivity and efficiency in farming operations.

Launched in 2014, SMAM aims to enhance the availability of modern technology to farmers, encouraging them to adopt mechanization for better agricultural practices.

Key Features:

  • Financial assistance: Provides subsidies for purchasing agricultural machinery, making it affordable for farmers.
  • Focus on small and marginal farmers: Special provisions are made to assist small and marginal farmers in acquiring modern equipment.
  • Training and capacity building: Offers training programs to educate farmers on the effective use of machinery and technology.
  • Encourages cooperative farming: Promotes the establishment of custom hiring centers for sharing machinery among farmers.

Sub-Mission on Seed and Planting Material (SMSP)

Objective:

The SMSP aims to enhance the production and availability of quality seeds and planting materials to farmers, ensuring better yields and improved agricultural productivity.

By providing access to high-quality seeds, the mission intends to increase the productivity of crops and improve the income of farmers.

Key Features:

  • Financial assistance for the production and distribution of quality seeds and planting materials.
  • Support for research and development in seed technology to enhance crop resilience and yield.
  • Promotion of seed replacement rates to ensure farmers have access to the latest high-yielding varieties.
  • Capacity building and training programs for farmers on the importance of quality seeds.
  • Collaboration with agricultural universities and research institutions for seed innovation.

Crop Residue Management Scheme

Objective:

The Crop Residue Management Scheme aims to promote the sustainable management of crop residue, reducing environmental pollution and improving soil health.

Launched to address the issue of stubble burning, this scheme encourages farmers to adopt eco-friendly practices for managing crop residues, thus contributing to cleaner air and improved agricultural productivity.

Key Features:

  • Subsidies on machinery: Farmers receive subsidies for purchasing modern equipment like happy seeders and mulchers to manage crop residue efficiently.
  • Training and awareness programs: The scheme includes training sessions for farmers on the benefits of crop residue management and how to implement these practices.
  • Financial assistance: Provides financial support to farmers adopting sustainable practices and investing in new technologies.
  • Reduction of pollution: Aims to decrease air pollution caused by stubble burning, promoting better environmental health.

Soil Health Card Scheme

Objective:

To promote balanced use of fertilizers based on the health status of the soil, ensuring long-term productivity and soil health.

This scheme was launched to provide farmers with detailed information about the health and fertility of their soil. The soil health card includes recommendations for fertilizer and nutrient use.

Key Features:

  • Detailed report on soil nutrient status with specific recommendations for fertilizers.
  • Promotes balanced fertilizer use to improve soil health and crop yield.
  • Periodic soil testing at government labs to monitor health and fertility.
  • Farmers receive customized recommendations to optimize their farming practices.

Other Important Schemes:

  • National Agriculture Market (e-NAM): A pan-India online trading platform that connects farmers with buyers, helping farmers get better prices for their produce by eliminating middlemen.
  • Gramin Bhandaran Yojana: This scheme encourages the creation of scientific storage capacity and quality control for agricultural produce to reduce post-harvest losses.
