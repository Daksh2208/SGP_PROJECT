Berseem Farming Guide

1. What is Berseem?

Berseem (*Trifolium alexandrinum*), also known as Egyptian clover, is a leguminous forage crop commonly grown for its high nutritional value. It is primarily used as livestock feed, improving soil fertility through nitrogen fixation.

2. Characteristics of Berseem

  • Appearance: Berseem is a lush, green plant that grows about 1-2 feet tall, with trifoliate leaves and clusters of small white or purple flowers.
  • Flavor: The leaves have a mild, sweet flavor, making them palatable for livestock.
  • Nutritional Value: Berseem is high in protein, fiber, and minerals, making it an excellent forage option for dairy and beef cattle.

  • Soil: It prefers well-drained, fertile soils with a pH of 6.0 to 7.5. Sandy loam soils are particularly suitable.
  • Planting: Sow seeds directly in the soil after the last frost, about 1/4 to 1/2 inch deep, with a seed rate of 15-20 kg per hectare.

4. Care for Berseem

  • Watering: Berseem requires regular watering, especially during dry spells. Ensure the soil remains consistently moist but not waterlogged.
  • Fertilization: Apply a balanced fertilizer, particularly nitrogen, to promote healthy growth and yield. Side-dressing during growth can enhance productivity.
  • Pest Control: Monitor for pests such as aphids and leafhoppers. Use organic pest management strategies to control infestations.
  • Disease Control: Diseases like downy mildew can occur; practice crop rotation and use disease-resistant varieties when possible.

5. Harvesting Berseem

Harvest berseem when it reaches a height of about 12-18 inches, usually 60-90 days after planting. Use a sickle or mower to cut the plants at the base, allowing for regrowth for multiple cuttings.

6. Uses of Berseem

  • Forage: Berseem is primarily used as high-quality forage for livestock, particularly in dairy and beef production.
  • Soil Improvement: As a legume, berseem fixes atmospheric nitrogen, enriching the soil and enhancing fertility for subsequent crops.
  • Cover Crop: It can be used as a cover crop to prevent soil erosion and improve soil structure during off-seasons.
