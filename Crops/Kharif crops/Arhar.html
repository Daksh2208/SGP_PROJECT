Arhar (Pigeon Pea) Farming Guide

Optimal Growing Conditions for Arhar

Climate

  • Temperature: Arhar thrives in warm climates. Ideal temperatures range from 25°C to 35°C (77°F to 95°F). It is tolerant to drought but sensitive to frost and excessive moisture.
  • Sunlight: Arhar requires full sunlight for proper growth and development. It is a day-neutral plant, meaning it flowers regardless of day length.

Season

  • Planting Season: The best time to plant Arhar is during the monsoon season, typically from June to July in tropical and subtropical regions.

Soil

  • Type: Arhar grows best in well-drained loamy soils, but it can tolerate a variety of soil types, including light sandy soils and heavy clays.
  • pH Level: The ideal pH range is between 6.0 and 7.5. Arhar can tolerate slightly acidic to neutral soil conditions.
  • Fertility: Arhar requires a moderate amount of nutrients and benefits from nitrogen-fixing bacteria in the soil. Adding compost or organic matter enhances soil fertility.

Fertilization

Types of Fertilizers

  • Organic Fertilizers: Organic matter such as compost, farmyard manure, and green manure are highly beneficial for arhar crops. They improve soil structure and nutrient availability.
  • Inorganic Fertilizers: Inorganic fertilizers, particularly nitrogen (N), phosphorus (P), and potassium (K), should be applied based on soil tests to avoid overuse.

Fertilization Schedule

  • At Planting: Apply a balanced fertilizer such as NPK (20:60:40) at the time of sowing.
  • Mid-Growth: Apply a nitrogen-rich fertilizer when plants reach 25-30 cm in height to promote growth.
  • Post-Bloom: Minimize fertilization during the reproductive stage to avoid excessive vegetative growth.

Planting and Care

Planting

  • Seeds: Arhar seeds should be sown at a depth of 5-7 cm, with a spacing of 25-30 cm between plants. Germination typically occurs within 7 to 10 days under warm conditions.
  • Moisture: The soil should be moist but not waterlogged at the time of planting. Arhar can tolerate drought but needs adequate moisture during the germination and flowering stages.

Weed Control

  • Manual Weeding: Regular weeding during the first two months is essential for controlling weeds and ensuring healthy growth.
  • Chemical Control: Pre-emergence herbicides may be used after sowing to suppress early weed competition. However, mechanical cultivation is preferable to avoid chemical buildup in the soil.

Common Diseases and Pests

Wilt Disease

  • Symptoms: Sudden wilting of plants, yellowing of leaves, and stunted growth.
  • Conditions: Fungal infections thrive in poorly drained soils and high humidity.
  • Control: Use wilt-resistant varieties and ensure proper soil drainage. Practice crop rotation to reduce pathogen buildup.

Pod Borer

  • Symptoms: Pod borers feed on developing pods, reducing seed quality and yield.
  • Control: Monitor crops for early signs of infestation. Apply biological controls like Bacillus thuringiensis (Bt) or neem-based pesticides.

Root-Knot Nematodes

  • Symptoms: Root galls, stunted growth, and poor plant vigor.
  • Control: Use nematode-resistant varieties or treat seeds with biocontrol agents. Practice crop rotation with non-host crops like cereals.

Harvesting and Post-Harvest Care

Harvesting

  • Timing: Arhar is ready for harvest when the pods turn brown and dry, usually 150 to 180 days after planting, depending on the variety and growing conditions.
  • Method: Harvest the mature pods by hand or using a mechanical harvester. Dry the harvested pods in the sun before threshing to extract the seeds.

Post-Harvest Care

  • Storage: Store the dried seeds in airtight containers in a cool, dry place to prevent insect infestation and moisture buildup.
  • Processing: Arhar seeds are typically split to produce dal, which is a common staple in many South Asian cuisines.

Arhar Farming Guide

1. Seasons for Growing Arhar

Arhar, also known as pigeon pea, is a warm-season crop that thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. It can be grown in various seasons depending on the region:

  • Monsoon: In India and other tropical regions, arhar is typically planted during the monsoon season (June-July) for optimal growth.
  • Post-Monsoon: In regions with mild winters, arhar can also be planted after the monsoon for a winter harvest.
  • Dry Season: Arhar is drought-tolerant and can be grown in dry seasons with minimal water requirements.

2. Soil Requirements

  • Type: Arhar prefers well-drained loamy or sandy loam soil with good organic content.
  • pH Level: The optimal soil pH is between 6.0 and 7.5, but arhar is known to tolerate slightly acidic to neutral soil conditions.
  • Preparation: Add compost or farmyard manure to enrich the soil and improve its fertility before planting.

3. Best Fertilizers for Arhar

  • Balanced Fertilizers: Use a balanced fertilizer with an NPK ratio of 20-20-20 to support healthy growth and development.
  • Organic Options: Apply compost or well-rotted manure to enhance the organic matter in the soil.
  • Micronutrients: Arhar may benefit from the addition of micronutrients like zinc and boron to improve plant health.

4. Common Diseases in Arhar

  • Wilt: A fungal disease that causes plants to wilt and die prematurely.
    Control: Use resistant varieties and ensure proper crop rotation.
  • Blight: Causes leaf spots and can lead to defoliation.
    Control: Apply fungicides and practice good field hygiene.
  • Pod Borer: Caterpillars that damage pods and reduce yields.
    Control: Use biological control methods or insecticides as necessary.
  • Root Rot: Affects roots, leading to stunted growth.
    Control: Ensure good soil drainage and avoid overwatering.

5. Cultural Practices for Disease Prevention

  • Crop Rotation: Practice crop rotation to reduce the buildup of pathogens in the soil.
  • Proper Spacing: Space plants adequately to promote air circulation and reduce humidity, which can lead to fungal infections.
  • Sanitation: Remove and destroy infected plant debris to minimize the spread of diseases.
  • Watering Techniques: Water plants at the base to avoid wetting the foliage and reduce the risk of fungal infections.
  • Resistant Varieties: Plant disease-resistant varieties of arhar for better management of common pests and diseases.

6. Growing Conditions and Care

  • Temperature: Arhar thrives in warm temperatures between 75-90°F (24-32°C) and can tolerate drought conditions.
  • Watering: Water regularly during early growth stages, but arhar is drought-tolerant and can withstand dry spells during maturity.
  • Spacing: Space plants 18-24 inches apart to allow for adequate root and canopy development.
  • Pest Control: Monitor for pests like pod borers and aphids. Use organic or chemical control methods as necessary.
