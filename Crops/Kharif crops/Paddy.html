Paddy Farming Guide

1. Seasons for Growing Paddy

Paddy, or rice, is grown mainly in tropical and subtropical regions with warm temperatures and abundant water. The main growing seasons for paddy are:

  • Kharif Season: Paddy is typically sown in the monsoon season (June-July) and harvested in October-November. This is the primary rice-growing season in India and many other regions.
  • Rabi Season: In some areas, paddy is also grown in the winter season (November-December) and harvested in March-April.
  • Summer Season: In some irrigated regions, paddy can be grown during the summer season, sown in February-March and harvested in June-July.

2. Soil Requirements

  • Type: Paddy thrives in alluvial, clayey loam, or loamy soils that can retain water. The soil should be fertile and have good water-holding capacity.
  • pH Level: The ideal soil pH for paddy is between 5.5 and 6.5, slightly acidic. However, paddy can tolerate a wide range of pH levels from 4.0 to 8.0.
  • Preparation: Paddy fields are traditionally prepared through plowing and puddling to maintain soil moisture and ensure good seedbed conditions.

3. Best Fertilizers for Paddy

  • Organic Fertilizers: Use farmyard manure or compost to improve soil organic matter and fertility during field preparation.
  • Nitrogen Fertilizers: Apply nitrogen fertilizers like urea or ammonium sulfate during the early stages of plant growth to boost vegetative growth.
  • Phosphorus and Potassium: Use phosphorus (superphosphate) and potassium (potash) fertilizers to enhance root development and strengthen plants, particularly during transplanting.
