Pigeon Pea Farming Guide

1. Seasons for Growing Pigeon Peas

Pigeon peas, also known as red gram, thrive in warm climates and are commonly grown in tropical and subtropical regions. The best seasons for growing pigeon peas are:

  • Monsoon: Ideal for sowing during the onset of the monsoon (June-July), which provides adequate moisture for early growth.
  • Post-Monsoon: In areas with extended rainy seasons, pigeon peas can be planted towards the end of the monsoon for a second harvest.
  • Winter: Pigeon peas can also be grown during the winter season in regions with mild temperatures.

2. Soil Requirements

  • Type: Pigeon peas grow well in well-drained loamy or clay soils with good organic matter content.
  • pH Level: The optimal soil pH is between 6.0 and 7.5, making it suitable for slightly acidic to neutral soils.
  • Preparation: Add farmyard manure or compost to improve soil fertility and structure before planting.

3. Best Fertilizers for Pigeon Peas

  • Organic Fertilizers: Use well-decomposed manure or compost to improve soil quality and promote growth.
  • Phosphorus and Potassium: Apply phosphorus and potassium fertilizers for root development and better yields.
  • Micronutrients: Supplement with zinc and molybdenum to improve plant vigor and enhance nitrogen fixation.

4. Common Diseases in Pigeon Peas

  • Wilt Disease: Causes wilting and yellowing of plants.
    Control: Use disease-resistant varieties and maintain proper crop rotation.
  • Stem Canker: Fungal disease causing lesions on stems.
    Control: Apply fungicides and remove affected plants promptly.
  • Leaf Spot: Results in brown spots on leaves, leading to defoliation.
    Control: Use appropriate fungicides and practice good field hygiene.
  • Pod Borer: Insects that attack pigeon pea pods, reducing yield.
    Control: Use biological or chemical pest control methods when necessary.

5. Cultural Practices for Disease Prevention

  • Crop Rotation: Rotate pigeon peas with non-leguminous crops to prevent soil-borne diseases.
  • Proper Spacing: Space plants adequately to promote air circulation and reduce disease risk.
  • Sanitation: Remove infected plant residues from the field to control disease spread.
  • Watering Practices: Avoid overwatering and ensure proper soil drainage to minimize fungal infections.
  • Resistant Varieties: Opt for disease-resistant pigeon pea varieties to minimize pest and disease damage.

6. Growing Conditions and Care

  • Temperature: Pigeon peas thrive in temperatures ranging from 68-95°F (20-35°C) and tolerate dry conditions well.
  • Watering: Water young plants regularly, but pigeon peas are drought-tolerant once established.
  • Spacing: Plant pigeon peas 18-24 inches apart to ensure proper growth and air circulation.
  • Pest Control: Keep an eye out for aphids, pod borers, and other pests, and apply necessary control measures.
