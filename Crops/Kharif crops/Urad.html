Urad Farming Guide

1. Seasons for Growing Urad

Urad, also known as black gram, is a leguminous crop that thrives in warm and tropical climates. It can be grown in the following seasons:

  • Kharif Season: Ideal for planting in the monsoon, especially in tropical and subtropical regions, as the crop requires a warm and humid environment.
  • Rabi Season: In some regions, urad is grown in the winter with minimal irrigation for a rabi harvest.
  • Summer: Urad can also be planted in spring for a summer harvest, particularly in areas with controlled irrigation.

2. Soil Requirements

  • Type: Urad grows well in well-drained loamy or sandy soils that retain moisture while ensuring drainage.
  • pH Level: The optimal soil pH is between 6.0 and 7.5 for best growth.
  • Preparation: Incorporate organic matter such as compost or green manure to improve soil fertility before planting urad.

3. Best Fertilizers for Urad

  • Organic Fertilizers: Well-rotted manure or compost enhances soil quality and provides necessary nutrients for urad.
  • Balanced Fertilizers: Apply a balanced fertilizer with moderate nitrogen, as urad can fix its own nitrogen but benefits from phosphorus and potassium.
  • Micronutrients: Ensure adequate micronutrients, particularly zinc and boron, for healthy plant development.

4. Common Diseases in Urad

  • Yellow Mosaic Virus: Causes yellow spots on leaves and stunted growth.
    Control: Use virus-resistant varieties and control insect vectors like whiteflies.
  • Powdery Mildew: Appears as white powdery spots on leaves and stems.
    Control: Apply fungicides and maintain proper plant spacing.
  • Root Rot: Fungal disease that causes roots to decay.
    Control: Ensure well-drained soils and avoid waterlogging.
  • Thrips: Tiny insects that feed on plant sap, leading to reduced yields.
    Control: Use insecticides or biological controls as needed.

5. Cultural Practices for Disease Prevention

  • Crop Rotation: Rotate urad with non-leguminous crops to reduce disease and pest buildup.
  • Proper Spacing: Maintain adequate spacing to ensure air circulation and reduce the spread of diseases like powdery mildew.
  • Sanitation: Remove diseased plants and residues to prevent the spread of infections.
  • Watering Practices: Avoid over-irrigation and ensure proper drainage to reduce root-related diseases.
  • Resistant Varieties: Use disease-resistant urad varieties to minimize the occurrence of pests and diseases.

6. Growing Conditions and Care

  • Temperature: Urad thrives in temperatures between 77-86°F (25-30°C) and can tolerate drought conditions.
  • Watering: Though urad is drought-tolerant, it requires regular watering during the flowering and pod-filling stages for optimal yield.
  • Spacing: Sow seeds 12-15 inches apart for proper growth and air circulation.
  • Pest Control: Monitor for pests such as thrips and aphids. Apply appropriate biological or chemical treatments when necessary.
