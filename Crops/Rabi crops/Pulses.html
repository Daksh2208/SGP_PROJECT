Pulses Farming Guide

1. Seasons for Growing Pulses

Pulses are versatile crops that can thrive in various climates. The best seasons for growing pulses are:

  • Spring: Most pulses are planted in early spring (March to April) after the last frost.
  • Fall: Some varieties can also be planted in late summer (July to August) for a fall harvest.

2. Soil Requirements

  • Type: Pulses thrive in well-drained, loamy soils rich in organic matter.
  • pH Level: The ideal soil pH for pulses is between 6.0 and 7.5, slightly acidic to neutral.
  • Preparation: Incorporate compost or well-rotted manure into the soil to enhance fertility.

3. Best Fertilizers for Pulses

  • Balanced NPK Fertilizers: Apply a balanced NPK fertilizer (10-10-10) at planting to provide essential nutrients.
  • Organic Fertilizers: Use compost or well-rotted manure to improve soil fertility.
  • Top Dressing: Apply nitrogen-rich fertilizers during flowering for improved pod development.

4. Common Diseases in Pulses

  • Root Rot: A common soil-borne disease affecting the root system.
    Control: Ensure good drainage and avoid waterlogging.
  • Powdery Mildew: A fungal disease causing white powdery spots on leaves.
    Control: Use resistant varieties and fungicides as necessary.
  • Leaf Spot: Caused by fungal or bacterial infections, leading to leaf discoloration.
    Control: Practice crop rotation and maintain good field hygiene.

5. Cultural Practices for Disease Prevention

  • Crop Rotation: Rotate pulses with non-leguminous crops to break disease cycles.
  • Weed Control: Maintain a weed-free environment to reduce competition and disease risk.
  • Sanitation: Remove diseased plant debris and practice good field hygiene.
  • Water Management: Water adequately but avoid overwatering to prevent root diseases.

6. Growing Conditions and Care

  • Temperature: Pulses grow best in temperatures between 65-80°F (18-27°C).
  • Watering: Maintain consistent moisture, especially during flowering and pod formation.
  • Spacing: Space pulse plants about 12-18 inches apart in rows that are 2-3 feet apart for optimal growth.
  • Pest Control: Monitor for pests like aphids and caterpillars, using integrated pest management strategies as needed.
