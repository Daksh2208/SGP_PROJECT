Peas Farming Guide

1. Seasons for Growing Peas

Peas are a cool-season crop that thrives in moderate temperatures. The best seasons for growing peas are:

  • Spring: Plant peas as soon as the soil can be worked, typically in early spring (March to April).
  • Fall: In some regions, a second crop can be planted in late summer (July to August) for a fall harvest.

2. Soil Requirements

  • Type: Peas thrive in well-drained, loamy to sandy soils rich in organic matter.
  • pH Level: The ideal soil pH for peas is between 6.0 and 7.0, slightly acidic to neutral.
  • Preparation: Incorporate compost or well-rotted manure into the soil to enhance fertility and moisture retention.

3. Best Fertilizers for Peas

  • Balanced Fertilizers: Apply a balanced fertilizer (10-10-10) at planting to provide essential nutrients.
  • Legume-Specific Fertilizers: Use fertilizers with added phosphorus to support root development.
  • Top Dressing: Apply nitrogen-rich fertilizers during flowering for improved pod development.

4. Common Diseases in Peas

  • Powdery Mildew: A fungal disease causing white powdery spots on leaves.
    Control: Use resistant varieties and apply fungicides as needed.
  • Root Rot: A disease affecting the root system, often caused by overwatering.
    Control: Ensure good drainage and avoid waterlogging.
  • Downy Mildew: A fungal infection that causes yellowing of leaves.
    Control: Improve air circulation and use resistant varieties.

5. Cultural Practices for Disease Prevention

  • Crop Rotation: Rotate peas with other crops to reduce disease risk and improve soil health.
  • Weed Control: Maintain clean fields to minimize competition for nutrients and moisture.
  • Sanitation: Remove debris and infected plants to prevent the spread of diseases.
  • Water Management: Water adequately but avoid overwatering to prevent root diseases.

6. Growing Conditions and Care

  • Temperature: Peas grow best in temperatures between 55-75°F (13-24°C).
  • Watering: Consistent moisture is important, especially during flowering and pod formation.
  • Spacing: Space pea plants about 2-4 inches apart in rows that are 18-24 inches apart for optimal growth.
  • Pest Control: Monitor for pests like aphids and pea weevils, using integrated pest management strategies as needed.
