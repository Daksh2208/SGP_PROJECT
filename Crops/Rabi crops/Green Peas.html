Green Peas Farming Guide

1. Seasons for Growing Green Peas

Green peas thrive in cool weather and can be planted in various seasons depending on the climate. The best seasons for growing green peas are:

  • Spring: Plant peas in early spring as soon as the soil can be worked (March to April).
  • Fall: In milder climates, peas can also be sown in late summer (July to August) for a fall harvest.

2. Soil Requirements

  • Type: Green peas prefer well-drained, loamy soil that is rich in organic matter.
  • pH Level: The ideal soil pH for green peas is between 6.0 and 7.0, slightly acidic to neutral.
  • Preparation: Incorporate compost or well-rotted manure into the soil before planting to improve fertility.

3. Best Fertilizers for Green Peas

  • Balanced Fertilizers: Apply a balanced NPK fertilizer (10-10-10) at planting to provide essential nutrients.
  • Organic Fertilizers: Use compost or well-rotted manure to enhance soil fertility and promote healthy growth.
  • Top Dressing: Side-dress with nitrogen-rich fertilizers during the growing season for improved yield.

4. Common Diseases in Green Peas

  • Powdery Mildew: A fungal disease causing white powdery spots on leaves.
    Control: Improve air circulation and use fungicides as needed.
  • Root Rot: Caused by overwatering, leading to wilting and plant death.
    Control: Ensure proper drainage and avoid waterlogging.
  • Pea Weevil: A pest that can damage seeds and pods.
    Control: Use traps or insecticides for effective management.

5. Cultural Practices for Disease Prevention

  • Crop Rotation: Rotate peas with non-legume crops to reduce the risk of soil-borne diseases.
  • Weed Control: Regularly remove weeds to reduce competition for nutrients and moisture.
  • Sanitation: Keep the planting area clean by removing debris and dead plants to prevent disease outbreaks.
  • Water Management: Water at the base of the plants and avoid wetting the foliage to minimize disease risk.

6. Growing Conditions and Care

  • Temperature: Green peas grow best in temperatures between 55-70°F (13-21°C) and are frost-sensitive.
  • Watering: Peas require consistent moisture, especially during flowering and pod development.
  • Spacing: Space pea plants about 2-4 inches apart in rows that are 18-24 inches apart for optimal growth.
  • Pest Control: Monitor for pests like aphids and cutworms. Employ organic pest control methods as needed.
