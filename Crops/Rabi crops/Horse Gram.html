Horse Gram Farming Guide

1. Seasons for Growing Horse Gram

Horse gram is a drought-resistant legume that grows well in warm climates. The best seasons for growing horse gram are:

  • Post-Monsoon: Horse gram is typically sown after the monsoon season (August to September) for a winter harvest.
  • Winter: In some regions, it can also be grown in winter (October to November) for harvest in early spring.

2. Soil Requirements

  • Type: Horse gram thrives in well-drained, sandy or loamy soils with good fertility.
  • pH Level: The ideal soil pH for horse gram is between 6.0 and 7.0, slightly acidic to neutral.
  • Preparation: Incorporate organic matter such as compost to improve soil structure and fertility.

3. Best Fertilizers for Horse Gram

  • Organic Fertilizers: Use compost or well-rotted manure to enhance soil fertility.
  • Phosphate Fertilizers: Apply phosphorus-rich fertilizers (like single superphosphate) at planting to promote root development.
  • Top Dressing: Use a nitrogen-rich fertilizer during flowering for increased pod and seed yield.

4. Common Diseases in Horse Gram

  • Powdery Mildew: A fungal disease causing white powdery spots on leaves.
    Control: Use resistant varieties and apply fungicides as needed.
  • Root Rot: Caused by waterlogged conditions leading to wilting and plant death.
    Control: Ensure proper drainage and avoid overwatering.
  • Stem Blight: A disease that affects the stems, causing them to decay.
    Control: Rotate crops and remove infected plants.

5. Cultural Practices for Disease Prevention

  • Crop Rotation: Rotate horse gram with non-legume crops to reduce soil-borne diseases.
  • Weed Control: Keep the field free of weeds to prevent competition for nutrients and moisture.
  • Sanitation: Remove plant debris and diseased plants to minimize disease spread.
  • Water Management: Water at the base of the plants and avoid excessive moisture on foliage.

6. Growing Conditions and Care

  • Temperature: Horse gram grows best in temperatures between 70-85°F (21-29°C) and is drought-resistant.
  • Watering: Water sparingly, as horse gram is drought-tolerant. Ensure moisture during germination.
  • Spacing: Space horse gram seeds about 6-8 inches apart in rows 1-2 feet apart for optimal growth.
  • Pest Control: Monitor for pests like aphids and caterpillars. Use organic pest control methods as needed.
